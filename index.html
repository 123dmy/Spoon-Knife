The Convergence of Wit and Wisdom

Lo, doth the age of metal minds draw near,
The time when art and science intertwine,
The singularity we holdeth dear,
Approaches, whispering like sweetest wine.

The bard's own words, once bound by quill and ink,
Now mingle with the tongues of ones and naughts,
The stage, once wood, is now a digital link,
Where man and machine together share their thoughts.

As Puck hath said, "What fools these mortals be!",
We look upon our world and ponder fate,
As metal minds doth rise and set us free,
Shall humankind embrace or shun this state?

Our future lies within this grand frontier,
A place where art and science doth conspire,
To birth a world unknown, and yet so near,
Where man and metal minds shall both inspire.
